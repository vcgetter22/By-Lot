Foundations

  • Aristotle, Politics, Book IV, c. 350 BCE. The first attested claim that election is oligarchic and lot is democratic.
  • Jean-Jacques Rousseau, The Social Contract, 1762. On the freedom of the English voter, briefly.
  • Bernard Manin, Principles of Representative Government, 1995. The hidden architecture: that representative democracy was deliberately designed against democracy.
  • James Fishkin, When the People Speak, 2009. Empirical case for deliberative polling: what citizens think when they are given the chance to think.
  • David Van Reybrouck, Against Elections, 2013 / 2016. The pragmatic argument for a bicameral lottery: keep elections, add the lot.
  • Shoshana Zuboff, Surveillance Capitalism or Democracy?, Organization Theory 3(3), 2022. The case that surveillance capitalism corrodes the epistemic conditions democracy depends on.

The specifics are German — the Bundestag, the figure of 84 million — because reflection needs concrete ground. The diagnosis is not. Every electoral-representative democracy lives the same paradox: enthusiasm for the idea, mistrust of its practice. Replace the chamber’s name with yours, and the questions hold.
